Tryps Brand Creative Notes

Creative recommendations from brand questionnaire review (87 questions)

Sean DeFaria March 19, 2026 For Jake Stein / Tryps
01

iMessage Agent: Kill the Chatbot, Keep the Channel

  • The iMessage travel agent concept is not it. A bot talking in your group chat feels gimmicky, which directly contradicts the brand values.
  • Instead: keep iMessage as the channel, but prioritize native polling only. Short, clean, declarative messages ("Pick your dates") with polls attached. No emoji, no personality, no agent "voice."
  • Think Uber-level communication. Uber texts you "Your driver is arriving" -- not "Hey! Your ride's almost there!" The iMessage layer is infrastructure, not a character.
  • The Postcard is the real product interface, not the chat.
02

The Postcard

  • When you send a Tryps link, it's called a Postcard.
  • It should be a reveal experience -- like opening a gift or chest. The destination reveal is an event, not just information.
  • Ties into real travel artifacts (people actually send postcards on trips). Implies something personal, not transactional.
  • The opening/reveal mechanic is built into what a postcard IS -- you flip it over.
  • Connects to the existing "trip card" concept but elevates it.
03

The Tryps Agent Character

  • The agent should have a distinct personality -- like a character, not a corporate travel bot.
  • Think: the most fun, well-traveled person from your college. Someone who brings raw energy without being gimmicky. Not a classic travel agent.
  • The character lives in the app, NOT in the group chat. In iMessage it's clean infrastructure. In the app, there's someone with a name, a vibe, a personality.
  • The agent should NOT be called "Tryps." The brand is bigger than the bot. It needs its own name (TBD).
  • Where the character appears: in-app chat, Postcard reveals, push notifications, Travel DNA results, trip suggestions.
04

Visual Identity & Campaigns

Instagram = The Storefront

  • The film camera / disposable aesthetic is a great content generation engine (TikTok, commercials, storytelling) but it is NOT the brand identity.
  • Instagram should be clean, professional, Apple-level. It sells an app. Polished product shots, real usage photography, everything intentional.
  • The grid stays pristine -- only high-production content Tryps produces. UGC lives in story highlights.
  • All user/seeded content lives everywhere else on the internet -- TikTok, Twitter, Snap, creator accounts. This makes Tryps feel organically everywhere without the brand account looking messy.
  • Same playbook as music artist seeding: the artist page stays clean, the network makes it feel like the world is talking about them.
  • Film camera photos need professional art direction if used in brand materials. Think "Shot on iPhone" -- real photos, immaculate presentation.

Campaign Direction: "See Your Friends More"

  • The emotional sell isn't "plan trips easier." It's "see your friends more."
  • Tryps removes the friction that keeps people apart. Campaigns show the outcome, not the tool.
  • Show the before (missing people, saying "we need to link up" for 2 years) and the after (actually there, actually together).
  • Jake said it himself: "I miss these boys. Tryps helps me do that." That's the campaign.
  • Your life is measurably better when you actually follow through. Happier at every age -- because you're connecting with friends.

Diversity is Non-Negotiable

  • Go Apple mode. Include everyone. The launch video should look like the actual diversity of a college campus.
  • The niche USC Indian friend group. The international students. The girls who travel every break. All different, all using Tryps.
  • This makes it feel like "the way everyone plans trips" instead of "for a certain type of person."
  • Also serves growth -- if only one demo is shown, virality is capped. If everyone sees themselves, every friend group thinks "that's us."

06

Stamps as Art Program

  • Each destination gets a unique stamp designed by a commissioned artist.
  • Similar to how beverage brands hire artists to design limited edition cans.
  • The stamp collection becomes a collectible layer people screenshot and share.
  • Scales naturally as Tryps grows into more cities/countries.
  • Minor feature, taken seriously in craft. Will hit hard.
07

Content Ratio: 80/20 at Launch

  • Recommendation: 80% lifestyle / 20% product, possibly 90/10 in the first month.
  • The marketing is the "why," not the "how." The app demo is the app itself. The launch video handles the experience walkthrough. Instagram sells the feeling.
  • You don't need to see how Uber works. You just want to travel quicker. Same for Tryps -- you just need to be reminded that you deserve to go see your friends.
  • As the brand matures with social proof, shift toward 70/30 or 60/40.
08

Campaign Ideas

"You Deserve It"

  • Permission-giving campaign. People already want to see their friends but feel like they can't justify it -- too busy, too expensive, too hard to plan.
  • "You Deserve It" cuts through all the excuses. Simple, emotional, universal.

GoPro Format for TikTok / Social

  • 5 seconds of the most captivating trip moment (cliff jump, group running into the ocean, surprise airport arrival) -- then hard cut to the Tryps logo.
  • No explanation, no feature walkthrough, no voiceover. Just the moment and the brand.
  • The viewer fills in the gap: "these people are having the time of their lives, Tryps is behind it."

"People Who Use Tryps vs People Who Don't"

  • Series format. One side: the group that actually went to Tulum. Other side: the group chat that's been saying "we should do something" for 3 years.
  • No app screenshots needed. Just the contrast. Everyone knows which side they're on.
09

Social Platform Priority

  1. Twitter -- discovery, reach, launch video lives here
  2. TikTok -- reach, raw content, creator-driven
  3. Instagram -- the storefront, not the discovery engine. People visit after they've heard of you.
  4. Pinterest
  5. Snapchat -- underrated. College kids document real lives here. Sponsored Tryps with influencers like Livvy Dunne. Feels native, not like an ad.
Note
Twitter and TikTok drive discovery/reach. Instagram is where people go to confirm the brand is legit. Different jobs.
10

Brand Essence: One-Word Association

  • "Welcome" (Jake's current pick) feels too Marriott. Too passive, too hospitality-corporate.
  • The energy should be spontaneous, committal -- the moment you stop overthinking and just commit.
Top Candidate
Reunion
Captures the real reason people plan trips -- to see their people again. Welcome says "come in." Reunion says "we're back."
When
Not if, when. The trip is happening. Only question is when.
Say Less
You don't need convincing. You don't need 47 texts. Say less. The trip is handled.
Bet
Someone sends the Postcard. "Bet." You're in. Done.
11

Brand Partnerships: Cultural > Corporate

  • The obvious plays (Airbnb, airlines, luggage, festivals) will happen and make sense.
  • What hits hardest is cultural collabs -- Tryps on a smoothie at Erewhon, unexpected streetwear crossovers, showing up in physical spaces where the demo hangs out.
  • A corporate partnership is a business play. A cultural collab makes Tryps feel like it lives in the world, not just on your phone.
  • The partnerships that matter most are the ones that make people say "wait, Tryps did THAT?"
12

Language: "Tryp" Replaces "Trip"

  • The goal is to replace the word "trip" with "Tryp" the way Uber replaced "taxi."
  • "Are you coming on the Tryp?" / "Who's on this Tryp?" / "That was the best Tryp."
  • The app is "Tryps" (plural, holds all your Tryps). Each individual trip is a "Tryp."
13

Funny / Viral Use Cases

  • Tryps needs a version of the Tinder/Hinge screenshot moment -- people using the app in unintended, hilarious ways and posting them.
  • The Postcard system shouldn't be limited to "real trips." Send one to the bar on Saturday, to your friend's couch, to the grocery store. The absurd ones get screenshotted and shared.
  • Easter eggs in the links. Funny edge cases. Lean into the humor on brand Twitter.
  • This is organic content that writes itself. People create the jokes, Tryps just enables them.

Back-Pocket Ideas

Launch Video
Consider a narrator in the launch video, like Axe cologne commercials. A confident, slightly irreverent voice guiding the story. Could pair well with "People Who Use Tryps vs People Who Don't."
Spyrt / Spirit Airlines
"Tryps" spelled backward is "Spyrt" -- close to "Spirit." Campaign idea for a tongue-in-cheek Spirit Airlines joke or "anti-Spirit" play.
FOMO Decline
When someone can't join a trip, their name gets crossed out with "FOMO" or similar. The decline should feel like a social moment, not a silent status change. Could tip gimmicky -- test the tone.
